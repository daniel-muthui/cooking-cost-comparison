## Interpretation notes

- Reproduction divergences on LPG and kerosene rows trace to the eCAP
  report itself: reverse-engineering Table 7 shows it costed LPG at
  ~172 KSh/kg and kerosene at ~169 KSh/kg, not the 230 / 203.06 its own
  assumptions table states. The engine is arithmetically consistent;
  the source report mixed price vintages.
- Table 7's electricity costs used the BASE lifeline charge only
  (12.23 KSh/kWh), understating true household cost by ~40%: pass-through
  charges and VAT were excluded. The tool uses the all-in effective rate.
- The engine's payback under eCAP assumptions lands in the same range as
  Figure 20; residual differences follow from the two points above and
  from Figure 20's unstated menu/tariff choices.
- Chapati-LPG REVIEW flag, new evidence: the eCAP report's own Table 7
  costs chapati-LPG at KSh 3.4 (~20 g of gas) while its energy table used
  the raw 161 g. The raw record is a transcription error; the true value
  is ~15-20 g (consistent with kerosene 22.5 g, ethanol 30 g). Confirm on
  field sheets, then correct via the EXCLUSIONS/patch list in
  clean_cct_data.py and rerun. Until then LPG-baseline savings are
  overstated by roughly KSh 140/month in the demo scenario.
- Chips-kerosene: report says KSh 30.8 but raw data (27.5 g) implies ~5.6;
  the report figure is unexplained by any price vintage. Engine follows
  the raw data.
